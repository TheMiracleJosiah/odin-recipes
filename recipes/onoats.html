Overnight Oats Recipe

Overnight oats are a no-cook, make-ahead breakfast made by soaking oats in milk or yogurt overnight, resulting in a creamy, nutritious, and customizable meal.

Overnight oats are a simple, no-cook breakfast option that involves soaking rolled oats in liquid—such as milk, yogurt, or a dairy-free alternative—overnight in the refrigerator. This process softens the oats, creating a creamy and pudding-like texture without the need for cooking. Typically, they are prepared in jars or airtight containers and can be customized with various sweeteners like honey or maple syrup, as well as mix-ins such as fruits, nuts, seeds, or spices like cinnamon. The beauty of overnight oats lies in their convenience, as they can be prepped in advance, making them a perfect grab-and-go meal for busy mornings.

Not only are overnight oats easy to make, but they are also packed with nutrients, offering a balanced combination of fiber, protein, and healthy fats. The slow absorption of carbohydrates from the oats helps maintain steady energy levels, while the added ingredients contribute extra vitamins, minerals, and flavor. They can be enjoyed cold straight from the fridge or slightly warmed if preferred. With countless variations, from chocolate banana to berry almond, overnight oats are a delicious and versatile way to start the day on a healthy note.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt (optional, for creaminess)
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds (optional, for thickness and nutrition)
  • 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional, for flavor)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon (optional, for warmth and spice)
  • 1/4 cup fresh or frozen fruit (e.g., bananas, berries, or apples)
  • 1 tablespoon nuts or seeds (e.g., almonds, walnuts, or sunflower seeds)
  • 1 tablespoon nut butter (optional, for extra richness)

Preparation Procedure

    1. In a jar or airtight container, add 1/2 cup of rolled oats.
    2. Pour in 1/2 cup of milk (dairy or non-dairy) and 1/4 cup of yogurt (if using).
    3. Add 1 tablespoon of chia seeds (optional) for extra thickness and nutrition.
    4. Sweeten with 1 teaspoon of honey or maple syrup (if desired).
    5. Stir in 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract and 1/4 teaspoon of cinnamon for extra flavor.
    6. Mix well to combine all ingredients evenly.
    7. Top with fruits, nuts, or nut butter for added texture and taste.
    8. Seal the jar and refrigerate overnight (or at least 4-6 hours) to allow the oats to absorb the liquid.
    9. In the morning, stir the oats, add extra toppings if desired, and enjoy cold or slightly warmed.
