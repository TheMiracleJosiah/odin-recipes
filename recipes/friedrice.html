Fried Rice Recipe

A steaming plate of fragrant fried rice, glistening with seasoning and garnished to perfection.

Fried rice is a beloved dish enjoyed worldwide, known for its savory flavors, satisfying textures, and versatility. Typically made by stir-frying cooked rice with a combination of vegetables, eggs, and proteins such as chicken, shrimp, or tofu, it is seasoned with soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and other aromatic spices. The dish originated in China but has since evolved into countless regional variations, from Thai pineapple fried rice to Indonesian nasi goreng. The key to perfect fried rice lies in using day-old rice, which is drier and helps achieve the ideal fluffy-yet-crispy texture when stir-fried at high heat.

This dish is a staple in many cuisines due to its ability to transform simple ingredients into a flavorful and comforting meal. It is often cooked quickly in a hot wok, allowing the flavors to meld while maintaining the distinct taste of each ingredient. Fried rice can be easily customized with different sauces, herbs, or chili pastes to suit various tastes and dietary preferences. Whether served as a side dish or enjoyed as a main course, fried rice is a go-to option for home cooks and professional chefs alike, offering a perfect balance of taste, convenience, and tradition.

Ingredient

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 2 cups chicken stock or water
  • ½ cup vegetable oil or butter
  • 1 cup diced liver (optional – can substitute with shrimp or chicken)
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1 cup sweet corn
  • 1 cup green peas
  • ½ cup diced green beans
  • ½ cup diced red and green bell peppers
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric (for color)
  • 1 teaspoon white or black pepper
  • 1-2 seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt to taste

Preparation steps

  1. Rinse the parboiled rice thoroughly and drain.
  2. In a pot, add the rice and chicken stock (or water) and cook until it's almost fully done but still firm.
  3. Drain any excess liquid and set the rice aside.
  4. In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  5. Add the chopped onions and minced garlic, then sauté until fragrant.
  6. Stir in the diced liver (or chicken/shrimp) and cook until browned.
  7. Add the diced carrots, green beans, sweet corn, peas, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for a few minutes.
  8. Season with curry powder, thyme, turmeric, black pepper, seasoning cubes, and salt to taste.
  9. Gradually add the cooked rice, stirring continuously to combine all the ingredients evenly.
  10. Allow the fried rice to cook for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors blend well.
  11. Remove from heat and serve hot with grilled chicken, fried plantains, or salad.
