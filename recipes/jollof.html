Jollof Rice Recipe

A vibrant plate of Jollof rice, rich in color and flavor, garnished with grilled chicken and fresh vegetables.

Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish known for its rich, smoky flavor and vibrant red-orange color. It is made by cooking rice in a well-seasoned tomato and pepper sauce, allowing the grains to absorb the deep, savory flavors. The dish is often prepared with onions, garlic, and a blend of spices, giving it a unique taste that varies slightly across different regions. Slow cooking ensures that the rice is infused with the sauce, resulting in a delicious, aromatic meal that is a staple at gatherings, celebrations, and everyday meals.

Typically served with grilled or fried chicken, fish, or beef, Jollof rice is enjoyed with sides like fried plantains, salad, or coleslaw. Some variations include adding vegetables, shrimp, or coconut milk for extra depth of flavor. While Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal each have their own versions of the dish, the debate over which country makes the best Jollof remains a fun and passionate topic across West Africa.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 3 large tomatoes (or 1 can of tomato puree)
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1-2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for spice)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 bay leaf

    • Preparation steps

    1. Blend tomatoes, red bell pepper, and onion into a smooth paste, then sauté chopped onions, garlic, and ginger in hot oil before adding tomato paste and cooking until reduced.
    2. Pour in the blended mixture, season with thyme, curry powder, smoked paprika, salt, and a bouillon cube, and let it cook until thickened.
    3. Rinse parboiled rice, add it to the sauce along with chicken or vegetable broth and a bay leaf, then cover and let it simmer on low heat for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally and adding more liquid if needed.
    4. Finally, mix in optional vegetables or proteins, let it rest, and serve hot with fried plantains, salad, or grilled protein.
